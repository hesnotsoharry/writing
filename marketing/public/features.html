Corkboard & outliner

See the whole arc at once

Zoom out and your scenes become index cards on a board — synopsis on the front, status colour at a glance. Drag them into a new order to fix the pacing, and the manuscript follows along. Prefer rows to cards? Flip the same view into the outliner: a sortable table of every scene's status, synopsis, word count, and labels. Cards for shuffling, the outline for hierarchy — one structure, two ways to see it.

  • Reorder scenes by dragging cards
  • Flip to the outliner — sort by status, words, or label, edit inline
  • Colour labels you define, shared across both views
  • Click any card or row to drop straight into the scene

Optional · $14.99/mo or bring your own

Use any model — ours, or your own.

The AI Writing Assistant is optional, off until you switch it on, and built so you stay in control of what it sees, what it costs, and where your words go.

Built-in subscription

Subscribe at $14.99/month and use Claude or ChatGPT immediately — no API account needed. You see the live cost before each run. A meter, not a bill; stops before it surprises you; resets monthly.

  • Claude (Anthropic) and ChatGPT (OpenAI)
  • Live cost meter before every run
  • Cancel any time — your writing never depends on it

Bring your own key

Enter your own Anthropic or OpenAI API key in Settings. Requests stream directly from your device to that provider — they never pass through our servers. No metering on our side; you pay your provider directly at their rates.

  • Your Anthropic (Claude) or OpenAI key
  • Direct device-to-provider — we never see the request
  • No metering from us — pay your provider, skip ours

Local or custom endpoint

Point the assistant at any OpenAI-compatible server — Ollama, LM Studio, or your own. When the model runs on your machine, your text never leaves it at all. Full privacy, no cloud, no per-use cost.

  • Ollama, LM Studio, or any OpenAI-compatible server
  • Fully local model — text stays on your machine entirely
  • No subscription, no metering, no internet required

Hide from AI — you decide exactly what it sees

Select any passage, mark it "hidden from AI," and it's replaced with a placeholder in everything sent to the assistant. The full text stays in your document, appears in exports, and counts toward your word count — the assistant simply never reads that part. Plain export and word count always use the full text; only the AI request is redacted.
