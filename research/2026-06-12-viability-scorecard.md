## Exploitable complaint gaps (review/community mining, 2024–2026)

1. **No AI integration** — fastest-accelerating complaint vs Scrivener; the wedge. (See positioning below.)
2. Steep learning curve — "cockpit of a 747."
3. Compile/export system — "requires a doctorate in molecular engineering"; the documented driver of switching to Vellum/Atticus.
4. Brittle Dropbox-only sync, no native cloud; conflict files across devices.
5. No real-time collaboration (blocks co-authors/coaches).

## Recommended pricing

**$14.99/mo** (above NovelCrafter's $14 top-tier-adjacent slot, below Dabble's $19, justified by AI-inclusive positioning) **+ $99 one-time** lifetime-updates tier (undercuts Atticus 33%; directly attacks Scrivener's paid-upgrade complaint). Revenue at 50/200/1000 customers: $750 / $2,998 / $14,990 MRR.

## AI positioning (market-side; feature spec is this repo's future work)

- **Dual positioning is mandatory — the writing world is polarized on AI** (anti-AI sentiment contributed to the NaNoWriMo org collapse). Frame: "a serious writing tool with AI you control," never "AI writes your novel." AI strictly opt-in + visible "never trains on your manuscript" promise.
- **Highest-leverage feature shape:** AI grounded in the writer's OWN worldbuilding notes/manuscript (consistency checks, brainstorm-against-your-codex, revision suggestions) — WritersNook's existing worldbuilding-notes structure is the natural substrate; this is NovelCrafter's proven killer feature.
- **Margin design:** subscription = managed fair-use AI credits; $99 one-time tier = BYO API key (user's cost, removes heavy-user margin risk). Model strategy: curated per-task routing (strong prose model + cheap utility model), NO model picker in the default UX; model selection only in the BYO-key power tier. Single provider day one behind a thin abstraction seam.
